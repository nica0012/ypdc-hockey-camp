Bio

Justin Stevens

Justin grew up playing for the Brantford 99ers, he is currently playing in Ottawa for the Carleton Ravens where they qualified for the CIS Nationals this season. Prior to this, Justin played NCAA Division 1 hockey at Mercyhurst University. He had a 5 year junior career playing in the GOJHL with the Brantford Golden Eagles, the OJHL with the Burlington Cougars and Hamilton Red Wings where he was an assistant captain and won the “Most Improved Player Award”. The CCHL with the Pembroke Lumber Kings, and the USHL, with the Youngstown Phantoms where he won the “Community Service Award”. He has one more year left at Carleton where he will graduate with a degree in Psychology and pursue a career in Professional Hockey.

Matt O'Connor

Matt signed a Two Year Entry Level Contract with the Ottawa Senators May 2015 NCAA Statistical Champion, with winning percentage of 88.1 Second in save percentage for all time Boston University Goaltenders. Recipient of the Regina Eilberg Scholarship Award 2013, 2015. Awarded to the student athlete that best combines excellence in both academics and athletics. He has worked as a Goalie School Instructor for Piero Greco and Shane Clifford, he also was a Youngstown Phantoms Youth Hockey Coach from 2010-2010. He Graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration from Boston University's Questrom School of Business 2015

Michael Stevens

Michael grew up playing minor hockey in Brantford for the 99ers and following that went on to play two seasons for the Ridley College Tigers prep school. Over the next couple of years he played Jr. A for the Georgetown Raiders, where he was named assistant captain in his first season, followed by the Stouffville Spirit, and finished playing Junior with the Kingston Voyageurs. Michael then went on to attend the University of Guelph where he currently plays for the Gryphons. He was fortunate enough to win an OUA Championship in his first season and finish third that season in all of Canada. He has two more years at Guelph where he plans to graduate with a Marketing degree and pursue a career in professional hockey.

Marc Stevens

Marc was born in Brantford Ontario and raised in Brantford and Turkey Point Ontario. Hockey has been his passion every since he was 3 years old, he grew up playing for the Brantford 99ers, then moved on to the Welland Tigers, and the Vaughan Kings. He was drafted in the 5thround of the OHL priority selection draft in 2012. He spent his first year of Junior hockey playing for both the Stouffville Spirit and the Guelph Storm. The next 3 full seasons he spent playing for the Guelph Storm in the OHL. His first full season in Guelph he was fortunate enough to win the OHL championship, and win the J. Ross Robertson Cup. This past year in Guelph he was given an assistant captain. Some personal awards that he has won are the Winmar Community Hero Award, the Humanitarian award, and the Leadership Award all this past year in Guelph. Marc still has one year of junior hockey left, where after that he aspires to play professional hockey.

Adam Brady

Adam grew up in Delhi Ontario and played hockey for his hometown Delhi Rockets before playing AAA in Brantford for Bantam and Minor Midget as a 99er. Adam was drafted 81st overall in the OHL draft by the Owen Sound Attack but decided to pursue an NCAA scholarship instead. Adam started playing junior A hockey at the age of 16 with the OJHL's Hamilton Red Wings, where he won the Rookie of the Year. The next season he played for both the Hamilton Red Wings and the GOJHL's Caledonia Corvairs before getting traded to the Kingston Voyageurs for the next two seasons, being an assistant captain his last year with the Vees. Adam finished off his final junior season this year with the Lincoln Stars of the USHL where he won the Chad Johnson Memorial Sportsmanship award. Adam intends on pursuing an NCAA scholarship and a professional hockey career following.

Matt Hoover

Matthew played his minor hockey career for the Brantford 99ers. Following that he attended Cushing Academy in Ashbrunham, Massachusetts for his grade 11 and 12 year, where he was the captain and won the championship in his second year. He then made the decision to commit to NCAA division 1 school Canisius College after the season. The next year he moved back to Ontario to play for the Kingston Voyageurs in the OJHL. This past season he had a breakout year and scored 38 goals and 43 assist for 81 points and tallying 100 penalty minutes to go with it. He will now look to carry his successful season into next year when he enters his freshman year at Canisius. He plans to major in sports management and plans to play pro hockey after college.

About YPDC

We are a group of local hockey players coming together to create a unforgettable experience for the kids. Our unique approach to building this camp maximizes the value for the Young Prospects having a high emphasis on skill development on and off the ice, mental strength training, off ice workouts, surprise NHL player appearances plus much more!

Our Camp Leaders have played at levels that consist of the NHL, AHL, OHL, NCAA, CIS, USHL and OJHL.
